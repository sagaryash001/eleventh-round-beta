# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Combat sports professionals — active fighters, managers, and promotions. They use this platform to manage their careers, find sponsors, track obligations, and access educational content. They are typically male, 18–35, accustomed to high-intensity environments. They view the platform primarily in evenings and at gyms (dark environments). Mobile and desktop split roughly 50/50.

### Brand Personality
Three words: **Disciplined. Uncompromising. Earned.**

The brand is not about flash or hype — it's about what's built through real work. Combat sports culture values authenticity above all. The name "Eleventh Round" refers to the championship rounds — where real fighters separate themselves.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark — always. These users are in dark gyms, late nights, high focus environments.
- **Color**: Near-black backgrounds (#0d0d0d to #141416), blood red accent (#8b0000), off-white text (#f0ece4). All tinted toward blood red. Zero pure black or white.
- **Typography**: Bebas Neue for display headlines (condensed, impactful). Barlow Condensed for UI labels and sub-headings. Barlow for body text. The existing font stack is appropriate and should be preserved.
- **Motion**: Purposeful. Slow reveals, staggered entrances on scroll. No bounce, no elastic. Smooth scroll via Lenis.
- **References**: Think Red Bull (editorial intensity), Represent (apparel brand gravity), UFC's darker editorial content. Anti-reference: generic SaaS dashboards, light-mode fitness apps, "clean and minimal startup" aesthetics.

### Design Principles
1. **Earn every element** — nothing decorative that doesn't serve the content or the user's focus
2. **Dark = premium here** — light surfaces feel wrong for this audience and context
3. **Typography does the heavy lifting** — large condensed type creates impact without gimmicks
4. **Restrained color** — blood red is used sparingly, never as a background flood
5. **Motion signals quality** — smooth scroll, staggered reveals, and purposeful transitions communicate craft without being showy
